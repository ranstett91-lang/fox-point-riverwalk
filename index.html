Vision

Turn Fox Point into a waterfront destination with a fundable environmental purpose.

The strongest version of this project combines public access, ecological repair, and stormwater or shoreline improvement. That gives Fox Point Riverwalk a clearer path to agency support and capital funding than a stand-alone park concept alone.

What The Site Needs To Show

  • A visible riverwalk vision that people can immediately understand.
  • Water-quality and resilience value tied to shoreline and runoff conditions.
  • A professional concept package that helps move toward implementation funding.

Funding Case

How to describe Fox Point Riverwalk for grant support

The framing should lead with eligible environmental outcomes and use the concept plan, site graphics, and info book as deliverables that advance a real project.

Lead With Water Quality

Position the riverwalk as a shoreline and stormwater improvement opportunity with ecological and runoff-management benefits, not only as a recreation investment.

Connect To Impaired Watersheds

Tie the work to nearby impaired watershed conditions, whole-basin planning, and the existing watershed-management framework where that connection is supportable.

Show Implementation Intent

Make the concept site plan, cost model, phasing, and professional booklet serve as tools for unlocking follow-on design and construction funding.
