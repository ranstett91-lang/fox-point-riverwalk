Site Context

A public river edge inside a hard-working industrial coast.

From Claymont through Fox Point and north toward the steel and port corridor, the Delaware River shoreline is low, altered, and shaped by rail, roads, fill, and heavy industry. Public access is rare. That is what makes Fox Point so important.

Fox Point can show how public access, shoreline repair, learning, and climate resilience can happen together in a place that has long been built for movement and industry.

Plain-Language Goal

Give people a safe and inspiring path to the river while making the shoreline stronger.

Source: 2022 Amtrak Climate Vulnerability Assessment Summary Report

Why Fox Point Matters

A local project inside a regional infrastructure story

At the river

Fox Point is one of the few visible public places on this stretch of shoreline.

Near major systems

It sits beside rail, highways, ports, and industrial land that are all under climate pressure.

Ready for a new role

That makes Fox Point a strong place to show what safer access and shoreline repair can look like.
