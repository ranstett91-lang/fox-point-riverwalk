Why This Matters To Amtrak

A real corridor problem needs a visible response.

Amtrak’s own climate work says this corridor is getting hotter, wetter, and more exposed. Wilmington is a sea-level-rise hot spot. Fox Point gives Amtrak a nearby place to make resilience visible and useful.

Core Message

Fox Point helps Amtrak solve a corridor problem in a way the public can see and value.

Why Now

Amtrak’s own data already makes the case

The reports do not need to be parsed line by line to show the takeaway. The corridor is under stress now, the pressure is growing, and Wilmington is part of that story.

450+

Weather disruptions

Amtrak recorded more than 450 climate-related disruptions from 2006 to 2019 across the corridor.

Source: 2022 Amtrak Climate Vulnerability Assessment Summary Report

$127M

Losses already felt

Those disruptions caused more than $127 million in losses, with even more losses expected ahead.

Source: 2021 Amtrak Climate Resilience Strategic Plan

Hotter by mid-century

Amtrak’s report says mid-century temperatures could rise by about 4 to 5 degrees in this region.

Source: 2022 Amtrak Climate Vulnerability Assessment Summary Report

Wetter by mid-century

Annual precipitation is projected to rise by about 5 to 15 percent across the corridor.

Why Fox Point

The right place to make resilience visible

Near the corridor

Fox Point sits close to the rail, river, and industrial systems already under climate pressure.

Public and legible

Unlike many infrastructure sites, people can see, use, and understand this place.

Ready to symbolize partnership

That makes it a strong site for Amtrak to show resilience leadership with public value attached.

Multi-Pronged Investment

What an Amtrak-aligned investment can buy

This is not only a riverwalk. The same investment can strengthen resilience, improve public experience, and create a stronger long-term funding story around the corridor.

1. Corridor resilience

Stronger shoreline thinking, water management, and site design that respond to a stressed edge.

2. Public-facing benefit

Trail access, overlooks, safer circulation, and a place people can connect to the river.

3. Stronger narrative

A public story that translates technical reports into trust, credibility, and momentum.

4. Funding leverage

A stronger concept package for grants, settlements, agency support, and future capital partners.

Pathway To Scale

How early capital can unlock larger capital

The early slide deck points to real funding pathways, but the real need now is a clean, powerful package that makes those pathways easier to activate.

Step 1

Lead with philanthropy

Fund the concept, visuals, engagement materials, and investment-ready package that turns Fox Point into a serious public project.

Step 2

Shape public asks

Use that package to pursue shoreline, restoration, transportation, and public-capital dollars with greater confidence.

Step 3

Unlock construction

Bring together layered funding for design, restoration, and public-facing riverwalk improvements over time.
